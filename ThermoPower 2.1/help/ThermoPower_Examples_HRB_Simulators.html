ThermoPower.Examples.HRB.Simulators.OpenLoopSimulator

Information


This model allows to simulate an open loop transient, starting from the guess initial conditions set inside the HRB model.


Simulate for 150 s. After about 50 s, the plant reaches a steady state. At time t = 50 s, the water valve is closed by 10%. At time t = 100 s, the gas flow rate is increased by 100%.



Connectors




TypeNameDescription


input RealInputGasFlowRateInput 


input RealInputValveOpeningInput 


output RealOutputTGoutOutput 


output RealOutputTWoutOutput 




Modelica definition


model OpenLoopSimulator
  HRBPlant Plant;
  Modelica.Blocks.Sources.Step ValveOpening(
    height=-.1,
    offset=1,
    startTime=50);
  Modelica.Blocks.Sources.Ramp GasFlowRate(          offset=10,
    height=1,
    startTime=100);
  Modelica.Blocks.Math.Add Add1;
  Modelica.Blocks.Interfaces.RealInput GasFlowRateInput;
  Modelica.Blocks.Interfaces.RealInput ValveOpeningInput;
  Modelica.Blocks.Math.Add Add2;
  Modelica.Blocks.Interfaces.RealOutput TGoutOutput;
  Modelica.Blocks.Interfaces.RealOutput TWoutOutput;
equation 
  connect(Plant.GasOut_T, TGoutOutput);
  connect(Plant.WaterOut_T, TWoutOutput);
  connect(Add1.u2, GasFlowRate.y);
  connect(Add1.u1, GasFlowRateInput);
  connect(Plant.GasFlowRate, Add1.y);
  connect(Plant.ValveOpening, Add2.y);
  connect(Add2.u2, ValveOpening.y);
  connect(Add2.u1, ValveOpeningInput);
end OpenLoopSimulator;








ThermoPower.Examples.HRB.Simulators.OpenLoopSimulatorHtc



Information



This model extends OpenLoopSimulatorSS, by computing the heat transfer coefficient to obtain an initial value of the gas outlet temperature equal to 125 degrees Celsius. This is performed by setting the fixed attribute of the Plant.Boiler.gamma_nom parameter to false, and by adding a corresponding initial equation to set the desired value of Plant.GasOut.T.


Simulate for 150 s. The transient starts at steady state, with the desired values of the heat transfer coefficient and gas outlet temperature. At time t = 50 s, the water valve is closed by 10%. At time t = 100 s, the gas flow rate is increased by 100%.



Connectors




TypeNameDescription


input RealInputGasFlowRateInput 


input RealInputValveOpeningInput 


output RealOutputTGoutOutput 


output RealOutputTWoutOutput 




Modelica definition


model OpenLoopSimulatorHtc
  extends OpenLoopSimulator(Plant(Boiler(gamma_nom(fixed=false))));
initial equation 
  Plant.GasOut.T = Modelica.SIunits.Conversions.from_degC(125);
end OpenLoopSimulatorHtc;








ThermoPower.Examples.HRB.Simulators.OpenLoopSimulatorSimplified



Information



This model extends OpenLoopSimulatorSS, with some modifiers to obtain a simplified version: the water medium model is replaced by a simpler one, the gas medium is assumed at fixed composition, and static balances are assumed for the gas side.

  extends OpenLoopSimulator(

ThermoPower.Examples.HRB.Simulators.ClosedLoopSimulator



Information



This model simulates a simple continuous-time control system for the HRB. The water outlet temperature is controlled to the set point by a PI controller, despite the disturbances on the water flow rate.


Simulate for 300 s. the system starts at steady state; at t = 50 s, the water flow rate is reduced by 10%; at t = 150 s, the water temperature set point is increased by 10 K.



Modelica definition


model ClosedLoopSimulator

  HRBPlant Plant;
  Modelica.Blocks.Sources.Step ValveOpening(
    offset=1,
    startTime=50,
    height=-.1);
  Modelica.Blocks.Continuous.PI TempController(k=0.3, T=12);
  Modelica.Blocks.Math.Feedback Feedback1;
  Modelica.Blocks.Sources.Step TWOutSetPoint(
    offset=330,
    height=10,
    startTime=150);
equation 
  connect(ValveOpening.y, Plant.ValveOpening);
  connect(TempController.y, Plant.GasFlowRate);
  connect(Feedback1.y, TempController.u);
  connect(Plant.WaterOut_T, Feedback1.u2);
  connect(TWOutSetPoint.y, Feedback1.u1);
initial equation 
  // Control system
  der(TempController.x)=0;
end ClosedLoopSimulator;







ThermoPower.Examples.HRB.Simulators.ClosedLoopDigitalSimulator



Information



This model simulates simple digital control system for the HRB. The water outlet temperature is controlled to the set point by a PI controller, despite the disturbances on the water flow rate.


Simulate for 300 s. the system starts at steady state; at t = 50 s, the water flow rate is reduced by 10%; at t = 150 s, the water temperature set point is increased by 10 K.



Modelica definition


model ClosedLoopDigitalSimulator
  HRBPlant Plant;
  Modelica.Blocks.Sources.Step ValveOpening(
    height=-.1,
    offset=1,
    startTime=50);
  Modelica.Blocks.Sources.Step TWOutSetPoint(
    offset=330,
    height=10,
    startTime=150);
  DigitalPI DigitalPI1(
    Kp=0.3,
    Ti=12,
    CSmax=11,
    CSmin=5,
    samplePeriod=0.4,
    CSstart=6.2,
    StartSteadyState=true);
equation 
  connect(ValveOpening.y, Plant.ValveOpening);
  connect(DigitalPI1.CSport, Plant.GasFlowRate);
  connect(TWOutSetPoint.y, DigitalPI1.SPport);
  connect(Plant.WaterOut_T, DigitalPI1.PVport);
end ClosedLoopDigitalSimulator;







ThermoPower.Examples.HRB.Simulators.ClosedLoopDigitalSimulatorSimplified



Information



This model extends ClosedLoopDigitalSimulatorSS, with some modifiers to obtain a simplified version: the water medium model is replaced by a simpler one, the gas medium is assumed at fixed composition, and static balances are assumed for the gas side.


These simplifications allow to run the simulation much faster than the original version.



Modelica definition


model ClosedLoopDigitalSimulatorSimplified
  extends ClosedLoopDigitalSimulator(
    Plant(redeclare package WaterMedium = Media.LiquidWaterConstant,
          redeclare package GasMedium =
            Modelica.Media.IdealGases.MixtureGases.CombustionAir(fixedX=true),
          Boiler(StaticGasBalances=true)));
end ClosedLoopDigitalSimulatorSimplified;
